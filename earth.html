Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known planet in the universe that supports life. It has a balanced combination of atmosphere, liquid water, and surface temperature that makes it uniquely habitable. About 71% of its surface is covered by oceans, while the remaining 29% consists of continents and islands. Earth’s atmosphere is composed mainly of nitrogen (78%) and oxygen (21%), which allows living organisms to breathe and protects the surface from harmful solar radiation and meteoroids. The planet has a moderate average temperature of about 15 °C, thanks to a natural greenhouse effect. Earth completes one rotation on its axis every 24 hours, creating the day–night cycle, and revolves around the Sun once every 365.25 days, resulting in the changing seasons. It also has one natural satellite — the Moon — which plays an essential role in stabilizing Earth’s axial tilt and generating ocean tides. Geologically, Earth is active, with tectonic activity, volcanoes, and earthquakes shaping its surface over time. Because of its diverse ecosystems, availability of fresh water, and protective magnetic field, Earth remains the most unique and complex planet we know.
