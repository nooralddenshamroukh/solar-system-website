Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest planet in our solar system, after Jupiter. Like Jupiter, it is a gas giant, composed mainly of hydrogen and helium, and has no solid surface. Saturn is best known for its spectacular ring system, which is the most extensive and visually striking of any planet. These rings are made up of ice particles, rock fragments, and dust, ranging in size from tiny grains to objects several meters across. With a diameter of about 120,536 km, Saturn is roughly 9.5 times wider than Earth, yet it is the least dense planet, with an average density lower than that of water. Saturn rotates quickly—completing one rotation in about 10.7 hours—and orbits the Sun once every 29.5 Earth years. The planet has a powerful magnetic field and an extensive system of moons, with more than 145 confirmed moons. Some of the most notable are Titan, which has a thick atmosphere and lakes of liquid methane, and Enceladus, which has icy geysers and a subsurface ocean that may be capable of supporting life. Saturn’s beautiful rings and diverse moon system make it one of the most fascinating and studied planets in our solar system.
