mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is often called the “Red Planet” due to its reddish appearance, which comes from iron oxide (rust) on its surface. It is a rocky, terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide. Mars is much colder than Earth, with average surface temperatures of about –60 °C, though it can vary from –125 °C at the poles to 20 °C near the equator during the day. The planet has a dry, desert-like landscape filled with dust, dunes, craters, and extinct volcanoes. One of its most famous features is Olympus Mons, the tallest volcano in the solar system, and Valles Marineris, one of the largest canyon systems known. Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos, and experiences seasons similar to Earth due to its axial tilt. Although it currently has no liquid surface water, scientists believe Mars once had rivers, lakes, and possibly even oceans, which makes it a strong candidate in the search for past life. Several spacecraft and rovers — such as Curiosity, Perseverance, and Ingenuity — are actively exploring Mars to study its geology, climate, and potential for future human exploration.
